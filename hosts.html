Web Hosting

Blue Host

Blue host starts at $2.95/month for the basic plan but renews at $11.99/month after the first month. This basic plan offers 10 websites, 10 GB SSD Storage, 100 Concurrent Visitors, AI Site creation tools, etc. They offer 3 other plans in the likes of Choice Plus, Online Store, and Pro. They recommend the Choice Plus, and so do I as it is the best bang for your buck. Starting ar $5.45/month then going to $21.99/month you get 50 websites, 50 GB SSD Storage, Free domain for the first year, etc. Overall, Blue Host offers a variety of plans good for many.

WP Engine

WP Engine offers the option to go monthy or annually. If you go annually you get a cheaper monthly price compared to if you go month by month. WP Engine offers three different plans for agnencies. These plans include the Managed WordPress: Growth, eCommerce Solutions: Growth, and the Agency Package. The cheapest option is the Managed WordPress: Growth plan at $77/month. This offers award winning 24/7 expert chat support, instant speed boost with EverCache and CDN, Proactive security, and free auto-renewing SSLs and daily backups. The Agency Package starts at $725/month and offers extra storage starting at 150 GB, smart plugin manager to keep sites secure, and Genisis Pro to build better websites faster. This hosting service is definitely geared towards larger businesses as you are spending quite a lot each month

GoDaddy

GoDaddys standard plan, the Web Hosting Economy starts at $6.99/month and offers 1 website, 10 databases, 25 GB of storage, free domain, free email, freee SSL Certificate, 30-day money back guarantee, etc. They offer other plans as well that range from $9.99/month to $21.99/month. The most expensive plan offers 50 websites, so if you are in need of that they have the options for it. Every plan includes, 1-click WordPress install, easy to use control panel, unmetered bandwidth, global data centers, and automatic daily backups.

My Recommendation

I personally would recommended GoDaddy as they have the cheapest plan that offers almost as much as BlueHost. WP Engine was in a realm of its own and not really comparable to the other two hosting sites. Blue Host seems like it would be cheaper, but looking deeper you see that they charge more after the first month. Go daddy is upfront with their prices, offer free domain, and is great for any size of business.
